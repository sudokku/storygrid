### DRAG-03 Requirement Deviation

REQUIREMENTS.md specifies **DRAG-03**: "Touch activation requires a 250ms press-and-hold with 5px tolerance." The implementation uses **500ms hold with 8px tolerance** — a deliberate UX decision made in CONTEXT.md D-07: "superseded by D-07 above which locks 500ms as the UX decision."

The plan claims DRAG-03 as a completed requirement, but the implementation intentionally keeps 500ms/8px rather than switching to 250ms/5px. This deviation is documented and intentional.

### Human Verification Required

#### 1. Ghost grab-point visual correctness

**Test:** Open the app in Chrome. Upload a media file to at least two cells. Create a cell that is taller than it is wide (e.g., split the root horizontally). Drag that tall cell by clicking near the bottom-center of the cell and holding for 500ms. Observe where the ghost appears relative to the pointer.

**Expected:** The ghost image should appear with the grab point preserved — the cursor should be approximately at the same relative position on the ghost that it was on the source cell. The ghost should NOT jump to center when drag activates, and the bottom-of-cell grab should show the cursor near the bottom of the ghost.

**Why human:** The grab-point offset correction depends on live `PointerEvent` coordinates, `draggingNodeRect`, and the `grabOffsetModifier` transform chain. The structural fix (matching ghost dimensions to modifier math) is verified; the perceptual correctness of the result requires visual confirmation with actual drag interaction.

#### 2. No accidental drag on quick mouse flick

**Test:** In the app (Chrome desktop), click and quickly move the mouse 8–10px on a cell (quick flick, under 500ms). Confirm that no drag initiates. Then click and hold on a cell for 500ms — confirm drag does activate.

**Expected:** Quick flick produces no drag. 500ms hold initiates drag with the cell dimming to 40% opacity and the ghost appearing.

**Why human:** Sensor constraint behavior (delay vs. distance) requires real pointer interaction with timing; cannot simulate the dnd-kit sensor constraint evaluation statically.
